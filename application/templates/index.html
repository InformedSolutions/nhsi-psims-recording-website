Record and Learn from Patient Safety Incidents

This online service will help you to gain access to records of patient safety incidents for the purposes of learning and safety improvement.

We'll use your information to create anonymised records of incidents that have occurred, which can be aggregated with other records nationally.

If you use an NHS Improvement account, you can return to access, and update, previously saved information.

Warning If your organisation has a Local Risk Management System, you should use that to record an incident, instead of this service.

You can use this service to:

  • record a patient safety incident that you were involved in or saw
  • obtain data about patient safety incidents nationally
  • access data about patient safety in your local organisation or trust
  • share learning and experience with other healthcare providers
  • understand current patient safety alerts and publications

Before you start

You will need information about the patient(s) involved, if you have access to it.

Why should I record patient safety incidents?

By recording patient safety incidents all NHS-funded care providers can learn from incidents across the healthcare system.

This service provides a method for people to record incidents such that they can be learnt from, without blame, in order to prevent similar incidents happening again.

How do I know patient confidentiality will be protected?

This service asks for information about the patient, so that we can identify trends in incidents for patients with particular needs.

However, no patient identifiable information should be recorded.

The national Patient Safety team in NHS Improvement use techniques to ensure that any inadvertently entered patient identifiable information is removed.

Who will be given the details of incidents that I record?

Information regarding those involved in an incident will not be shared beyond those with a national remit for learning from incidents (including NHS Improvement, CQC and MHRA).

This service does not replace local practices for investigating and managing incidents.
